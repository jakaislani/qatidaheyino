Renée Fleming Education

Fleming is a highly educated and qualified person. She studied at Churchville-Chili High School under the tutelage of Rob Goodling who taught orchestra, chorus, voice, theory/composition, and music history. She then attended the Crane School of Music at the State University of New York at Potsdam.

Fleming took up singing with a jazz trio while at SUNY Potsdam, in an off-campus bar called Alger’s. The jazz saxophonist Illinois Jacquet invited her on tour with his big band but instead chose to continue her graduate studies with voice teacher John Maloy at the Eastman School of Music in Rochester, New York.

As a student, Fleming spent several summers at the Festival and School of Music in Aspen (AMFS), where she studied with Jan DeGaetani and was directed by Edward Berkeley.

Fleming received a Fulbright Scholarship, which allowed her to work with Arleen Augér and Elisabeth Schwarzkopf in Europe. She then sang at the Juilliard School‘s jazz clubs to pay for further studies.
