Hiroshi Morie Died Battling Cancer

Heath X Japan Death: The untimely passing of the longtime bassist of X-Japan has died after a battle with cancer.

Heath, whose real name was Hiroshi Morie, entered the music scene in 1992 as a replacement for Taiji Sawada in the iconic band X Japan.

With Sawada’s departure due to creative differences, Heath swiftly became an indispensable member of the group.

His exceptional bass-playing skills and magnetic stage presence significantly contributed to the band’s success.

He left an indelible mark on X Japan’s discography, actively participating in the creation of albums like “Dahlia” and “The Last Live” and joining them on numerous tours and concerts.

Recognized for his talent, charismatic aura, long hair, and tattoos, Heath helped X Japan achieve both national and international acclaim, especially during the late 1980s and early 1990s.

His seamless integration into the band solidified their status as musical pioneers, leaving a lasting legacy in the world of rock and metal.

Heath X Japan Death: A Sudden End To A Brilliant Career

Heath, the bassist of the legendary Japanese rock band X Japan, has passed away at the age of 55 after a year-long battle with cancer.

According to sources, Heath was diagnosed with cancer earlier this year but kept his condition private from the public and even from his bandmates.

He reportedly wanted to focus on his treatment and recovery without causing any trouble or worry to anyone.

However, his health deteriorated rapidly in the past few weeks, and he passed away on November 7th, 2023.
