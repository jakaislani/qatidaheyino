Is Brendan Kavanagh Arrested? What Did Boogie Woogie Pianist Do?

Why was Brendan Kavanagh arrested? The famous British Boogie Woogie pianist has recently uploaded a video stating why he was nearly apprehended.

Brendan Kavanagh, popularly called “Dr. K,” is a British pianist and piano teacher with Irish ancestry.

He is recognized for his expertise in the boogie-woogie genre, which he passionately performs and promotes.

Brendan’s musical style primarily revolves around improvisation, combining elements of classical, jazz, blues, rock & roll, and traditional Irish music.

Kavanagh has gained significant popularity through his YouTube channel, named BrendanKavanaghDrK, which boasts an impressive subscriber base of 1.95 million.

Through his channel, he shares his musical talents, piano tutorials, and engaging performances, captivating a wide audience with his unique style and musical fusion.

Nevertheless, a week before, the noted pianist was almost arrested for a gig he was performing. Why so? Keep reading to find the answer.

Is Brendan Kavanagh Arrested? The YouTube Video Explained

No, Brendan Kavanagh is not arrested. The piano player was, however, cordoned off from the train station where he was playing a week ago.

The fans of Brendan Kavanagh must be familiar with the new video where Brendan played the piano and a jolly Irish man named Nick performed to the tune.

The video filmed at a train station has been uploaded under the title Dude Does Insane Aerobic Workout To Boogie Woogie. 

After hearing the Boogie Woogie tune, the passenger, Nick, portrayed a magnificent piece sporting aerobics and an intense cardio workout.

In addition, it is not the first time Dr. K and Nick have been featured together. The loyal followers of Kavanagh trace this duo long back, often collaborating.

Nick is an incredible individual with an immense talent for dancing and singing on stage. He seems to be born for the theater, showcasing his remarkable abilities.

Nevertheless, the gig did not go exactly as planned. While Brendan was playing in the station, the police arrived and gripped him, asking him not to play there.

According to Kavanagh’s new YouTube video describing what happened, the police held him tight and even pushed him out of the train station.

Brendan Kavanagh: What Did Boogie Woogie Pianist Do? 

Boogie Woogie pianist Brendan Kavanagh has not committed any crime; the artist was forced to leave the train station premise by a policeman.

Born in London, United Kingdom, in 1967, Brendan holds a Ph.D. in English, which accounts for his honorary title of “Dr K.”

The pianist pursued his education at reputable institutions, including University College Dublin, Middlesex University London, and University College Cork.

Kavanagh is recognized for his distinct approach to presenting himself.

In 2017, the talented street performer surprised and captivated commuters with his impressive piano skills while posing as a Metropolitan Police officer.

He shared the footage of his remarkable performances online, fooling thousands of viewers.

Brendan, hailing from Wealdstone, used various disguises to showcase his musical talent at train stations and famous landmarks throughout London.

One of his notable videos depicts him dressed as a police officer at Canary Wharf, which has garnered nearly two million views on Facebook since its recent posting.

The video garnered widespread acclaim for the officer’s impressive speed and skills, with netizens seemingly unaware that the musician behind the performance was him.
