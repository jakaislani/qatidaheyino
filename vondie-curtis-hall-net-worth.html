What is Vondie Curtis-Hall’s Net Worth and Salary?

Vondie Curtis-Hall, an American actor and director, has a net worth of $4 million. He is best known for his role as Dr. Dennis Hancock on “Chicago Hope” and has appeared in over 100 episodes of the series. Curtis-Hall began as a stage actor and was a part of the original cast of “Dreamgirls” on Broadway.

His filmography includes “Coming to America,” “Passion Fish,” “Eve’s Bayou,” and “William Shakespeare’s Romeo + Juliet.” He also directed episodes of several TV series, including “ER,” “The Shield,” and “Gossip Girl.”

Curtis-Hall has won multiple awards, including two Black Reel Awards, a Gotham Award, and a Satellite Award. He has also been nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award and three Screen Actors Guild Awards.

Early Life

Vondie Curtis-Hall was born on September 20, 1950 in Detroit, Michigan to Angeline and Curtis Hall. His mother worked as a nurse while his father owned a construction company. He grew up with his brother, Kevan, and sister, Sherrie. Curtis-Hall attended Presentation Our Lady of Victory grade school in Detroit where he was taught by the Oblate Sisters of Providence, the only all black order of nuns in the United States.

Vondie Curtis-Hall’s Career Highlights

Vondie Curtis-Hall began his acting career on stage in 1981 and appeared on screen in television and film in 1989. He developed his career throughout the 1990s, appearing in episodes of shows like “China Beach,” “The Trials of Rosie O’Neill,” and “ER.” One of his big breaks came in 1995 when he was cast as Dr. Dennis Hancock on the show “Chicago Hope,” which he remained on through the fifth season in 1999.

During the 2000s, Curtis-Hall appeared in films like “Turn It Up,” “Talk to Me,” and “Honeydripper,” and television movies like “Freedom Song” and “Deceit.” He continued to develop a reputation as a strong character actor throughout the 2010s, appearing in shows like “Law & Order: Special Victims Unit” and “Chasing Life.”

In 2015, he booked the main cast role of Ben Urich in the Marvel Cinematic Universe show “Daredevil.” He then appeared in the legal drama “For the People” from 2018 to 2019, and played the role of Reverend Green in the film “Harriet” in 2019.

Curtis-Hall has also directed episodes of shows like “ER,” “Gossip Girl,” and “The Starter Wife,” as well as television films like “Toni Braxton: Unbreak My Heart” and “Faith Under Fire: The Antoinette Tuff Story.”

Personal Life and Career
