What is Remy Ma’s Net Worth?

Remy Ma, a Grammy-nominated American rapper and actress, has an estimated net worth of $4 million.

Highlights

– Former member of Big Pun and Fat Joe’s rap crew, Terror Squad.
– One of only six female rappers to have a #1 single on the “Billboard” Hot 100 chart.
– Grammy-nominated for her contributions to the single “Lean Back.”
– Released solo studio album “There’s Something About Remy: Based on a True Story” in 2006.
– Joined the cast of the VH1 reality series “Love & Hip Hop: New York” in 2015.
– Guest-starred on “Empire” in 2017 and “Queen of the South” in 2018.
– Hosted the late-night talk show “State of the Culture” from 2018 to 2019.
– Starred on the spin-off “Remy & Papoose: Meet the Mackies” in 2018 alongside her husband, rapper Papoose.

Early Life

Remy Ma, born Reminisce Smith on May 30, 1980, in The Bronx, New York City, grew up in a family struggling with drug abuse. She took on the responsibility of caring for her siblings at a young age and expressed her experiences through poetry. Remy caught the attention of rapper Big Pun, who became her mentor after a meeting and freestyle session. She appeared on Big Pun’s album “Yeeeah Baby,” using the name Remy Martin.

Career

After Big Pun’s passing, Remy Ma was signed to Fat Joe’s label, Terror Squad Entertainment. She performed on the 2004 Terror Squad album “True Story,” and the single “Lean Back” achieved immense success, reaching #1 on numerous charts. Remy’s solo studio album, “There’s Something About Remy: Based on a True Story,” was released in 2006 and featured several notable singles. Remy’s association with Universal and Fat Joe became strained, leading to her departure from the label and the Terror Squad.

While Remy faced legal troubles and incarceration for the shooting of Makeda Barnes-Joseph in 2007, she continued to release mixtapes and music. After her release from prison in 2014, she returned to the music scene with new projects and also starred on “Love & Hip Hop: New York” alongside her husband, Papoose. Remy Ma has collaborated with various artists and released several singles over the years.

Personal Life

In July 2007, Remy Ma turned herself into the NYPD following a confrontation that resulted in the shooting of Makeda Barnes-Joseph, who had allegedly stolen from her. Remy was convicted of illegal weapon possession, assault, and attempted coercion. While in prison, she married Papoose over the phone in 2008. Remy was released in 2014 and held a wedding ceremony with Papoose in 2015. They have a daughter together, and Remy is also a stepmother to Papoose’s three children from previous relationships.

Remy Ma launched her clothing line, The Remy Ma Collection, in 2018, with a portion of the proceeds dedicated to the Remy Ma Foundation, supporting incarcerated women. She has faced legal issues, including assault accusations, but charges were later dropped due to lack of evidence.
